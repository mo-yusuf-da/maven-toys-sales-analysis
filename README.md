# Maven Toys — Sales, Profitability & Inventory Analysis

**Tool:** Power BI | **Domain:** Retail / Toys | **Period:** January 2022 – September 2023

---

## Project Overview

End-to-end analysis of Maven Toys, a fictitious 50-store toy retail chain in Mexico, covering 829,262 point-of-sale transactions across 35 SKUs in 5 product categories. The project answers Maven Analytics' four guided-project questions: category profitability by location, seasonal sales patterns, stockout-driven revenue loss, and inventory carrying cost — combining a Power BI dashboard with a full written business analysis report.

---

## Dataset

| Table | Records | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Sales | 829,262 | Transaction-level order data, Jan 2022 – Sep 2023 |
| Products | 35 | SKU, category, cost, price |
| Stores | 50 | City, location type (Downtown/Commercial/Residential/Airport), open date |
| Inventory | 1,593 | Current stock-on-hand by store × product |

---

## Key Findings

- **$14.44M total revenue** and **$4.01M gross profit** (27.8% margin) across the full period
- **Electronics is the margin leader** at 44.6% margin from just 3 SKUs — nearly double the margin of Toys, the highest-revenue category
- **Downtown stores outperform every other location type in every single product category** — the strongest, most consistent profit driver in the dataset
- **December is the peak month every year**, with a predictable July–August seasonal trough
- **Weekends drive 39% of revenue** from just 2 of 7 days (Friday–Saturday)
- **Toys has the highest stockout rate (7.4%)** of any category — and it's also the highest-revenue category, making it the single biggest source of identified lost sales
- **Inventory is unevenly distributed**: some SKUs carry under 7 days of supply while others sit at nearly 58 days
